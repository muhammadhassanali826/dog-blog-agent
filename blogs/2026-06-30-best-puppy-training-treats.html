The Ultimate Guide to the Best Puppy Training Treats

puppy training treats Positive Reinforcement Healthy Rewards
Welcoming a new puppy into your home is an incredibly exciting journey filled with soft cuddles, play sessions, and—of course—plenty of training. To set your new companion up for success, choosing the right training tools is key. The right puppy training treats can make all the difference in helping your pup learn commands quickly and happily.

Quick Answer: What Makes the Best Puppy Training Treat?

The best puppy training treats are small, soft, highly aromatic, and easy to digest. Because training requires frequent repetition, treats should be low in calories to avoid weight gain and formulated with simple, high-quality ingredients to protect your puppy's sensitive, developing digestive system.

Why the Right Puppy Training Treats Matter

When training a puppy, you aren't just giving them a snack; you are communicating with them. According to the ASPCA's guide on common dog behavior issues, positive reinforcement—rewarding behaviors you want to see repeated—is the most effective way to shape your puppy's habits.

Using standard kibble or oversized, crunchy biscuits for training often falls short. A great training treat needs to be consumed quickly so your puppy doesn't lose focus while chewing. Additionally, because you will be delivering multiple rewards in a single session, these treats must be healthy and portion-controlled to prevent digestive upset or unwanted weight gain.

What to Look For in a Puppy Training Treat

Not all dog treats are designed with training in mind. To keep your puppy motivated and healthy, look for these key characteristics:

  • Small Size: Treats should be roughly the size of a pea. This allows your pup to swallow the reward instantly and remain focused on you.
  • Strong Aroma: Puppies navigate the world through scent. A smelly treat is a high-value treat, which is incredibly useful when working around distractions.
  • Soft Texture: Soft treats are easier and faster to chew than hard biscuits, keeping the momentum of your training session going.
  • Clean Ingredients: Avoid artificial fillers, chemical preservatives, and excessive salt or sugars. Natural ingredients support healthy growth and are much gentler on sensitive puppy stomachs.

Understanding Treat Values

To train effectively, it helps to categorize your treats by "value." This helps you decide which treat to use depending on the difficulty of the task and the level of distraction.

Puppy Training Tips: Using Treats Wisely

Having the best puppy training treats is only half the battle. How you use them makes all the difference in your puppy's learning curve. Keep these expert training tips in mind:

1. Follow the 10% Rule

To keep your puppy healthy, make sure treats do not exceed 10% of their total daily food intake. According to the American Kennel Club (AKC), over-treating can lead to nutritional imbalances and weight issues. If you have an intensive training day, consider reducing your puppy's dinner portion slightly to compensate.

2. Keep Training Sessions Short and Sweet

Puppies have incredibly short attention spans. Aim for training sessions that last just 5 to 10 minutes, several times a day. End on a high note with a successful command and a jackpot reward (several treats at once) to keep them excited for the next session.

3. Vary Your Rewards

Just like humans, puppies can get bored of eating the exact same thing every day. Rotating your training treats keeps things fresh and exciting. Take a look at our Dog treat rotation guide to learn how to keep your puppy eagerly guessing what reward is coming next.

4. Watch for Digestive Changes

Because your puppy's digestive system is still developing, look out for signs of stomach sensitivity, such as loose stools, vomiting, or diarrhea. If you notice persistent issues, consult your veterinarian to rule out food sensitivities or allergies.

Frequently Asked Questions

When can puppies start having training treats?

Most puppies can start receiving soft, natural training treats around 8 weeks of age, which is typically when they transition to solid food. Always choose treats specifically formulated for young puppies and introduce new foods slowly.

How many training treats can I give my puppy a day?

While there is no single number, treats should never make up more than 10% of your puppy's daily calorie intake. If you are doing a lot of training, break treats into tiny, pea-sized crumbs to stretch them further without overfeeding.

What are high-value training treats?

High-value treats are highly aromatic, delicious rewards that your puppy rarely gets. These are reserved for difficult commands (like recall) or highly distracting environments (like the park). Single-ingredient options like freeze-dried beef lung are excellent examples.

Can training treats cause my puppy to get diarrhea?

Yes, sudden dietary changes or overfeeding can upset a puppy's sensitive stomach, leading to loose stools or diarrhea. Always introduce new treats gradually, and consult your veterinarian if your puppy experiences persistent digestive issues, vomiting, or lethargy.
