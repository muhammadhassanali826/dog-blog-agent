Puppies are naturally curious and explore the world with their mouths. This is especially true when they are teething. Their gums can become sore and itchy, and chewing provides much-needed relief. Beyond teething, chewing is a vital part of a puppy's development and well-being. It helps them:

  • Learn appropriate chewing behavior.
  • Maintain good dental hygiene.

Choosing the Right Bully Stick Size for Your Puppy

Selecting the correct size bully stick is crucial for your puppy's safety and enjoyment. A chew that is too small can be a choking hazard, while one that is too large might be overwhelming or too difficult for them to manage. At Brutus & Barnaby, we offer a variety of sizes to accommodate growing pups.

For puppies, it's generally recommended to start with smaller or regular-sized bully sticks. As your puppy grows and becomes a more experienced chewer, you can gradually introduce larger sizes. Always supervise your puppy during chew time, regardless of the size of the chew.

Important Considerations for Puppy Chewing

While bully sticks are a wonderful choice, responsible pet ownership includes understanding safe chewing practices. Always supervise your puppy when they have a chew toy or treat. This allows you to intervene if they try to swallow large pieces or choke.

It's also important to monitor your puppy's diet and treat intake. Treats should not make up more than 10% of their daily caloric intake. Overfeeding treats can lead to weight gain and nutritional imbalances. For more guidance on treat moderation, consult resources like the AKC's guide on treat allowances.

If your puppy exhibits sudden changes in behavior, develops allergies, or shows signs of digestive upset (like vomiting or diarrhea), it's always best to consult your veterinarian. They can provide personalized advice based on your puppy's specific health needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Puppy Bully Sticks

Q1: Are bully sticks safe for teething puppies?

Yes, bully sticks are generally safe and beneficial for teething puppies. They provide a durable, satisfying chew that can help soothe sore gums and redirect chewing from inappropriate items. Always choose an appropriate size and supervise.

Q2: How long does a bully stick last for a puppy?

The duration a bully stick lasts depends on your puppy's chewing intensity and the size of the stick. For puppies, a standard 5-6 inch bully stick can last anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours of active chewing. Some puppies may finish them faster, while others will savor them over multiple sessions.

Q3: Can puppies eat bully sticks every day?

While bully sticks are digestible, it's best to offer them in moderation. As part of a balanced diet, they can be given several times a week. Avoid making them a daily staple to ensure your puppy receives a variety of nutrients and doesn't consume too many calories from treats alone. Consult the AKC guidelines for treat recommendations.

Q4: What's the difference between bully sticks and rawhide?

Bully sticks are made from beef muscle and are highly digestible. Rawhide is made from the inner layer of cow or pig hides and can be harder to digest, posing a higher risk of obstruction if large pieces are swallowed. Bully sticks are generally considered a safer, more natural alternative.

Q5: When should I take away a bully stick from my puppy?

You should take away a bully stick when it becomes small enough to be swallowed whole or when your puppy starts to break off large chunks. This is a crucial safety measure to prevent choking hazards. Always supervise your puppy closely while they chew.
